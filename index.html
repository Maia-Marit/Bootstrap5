Facts About Dandelions

─•~❉᯽❉~•─

Seed travelers:

Each puffball can release up to 200 seeds, and those seeds can travel up to 5 miles (8 km) in the wind.


─•~❉᯽❉~•─

Natural soil healers:

Their deep roots pull minerals like calcium and potassium to the surface, enriching the soil when they die and decompose.


─•~❉᯽❉~•─

Name origin:

“Dandelion” comes from the French dent de lion — meaning “lions tooth,” referring to the jagged edges of the leaves.


─•~❉᯽❉~•─

Pet-friendly snack:

Rabbits, guinea pigs, and tortoises love dandelions — they are like the plant version of potato chips for small animals.


─•~❉᯽❉~•─

Wishing flower!

Blowing on a dandelion puffball and watching the seeds float away is an old way to make a wish — each seed supposedly carries it into the universe.
